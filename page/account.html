Privacy Statement

Last changed on: 2016-02-01

Knowing an API key allows anyone to view your account's ingame information, such as: characters and guilds, bank and inventory contents, gold and assets, achievements and unlocks, Trading Post and PvP match history, and playtime and activity. You may limit which of these type of information is exposed when creating an API key, and disallow further access to these information by deleting the API key at the ArenaNet website.

GW2Timer.com does not view, store, collect, monitor, or datamine API keys or your account information. When you enter the API key into the website, you will be downloading data from ArenaNet servers and performing calculations using this web application locally on your computer, and the keys are stored in your web browser, not a server.

GW2Timer.com uses Google Analytics and is able to view and store text that is shown on your address bar when you are on this website. If you do not wish to expose your API key, do not put your API key in the browser address bar. To share your key with other people, private message them the key and direct them to gw2timer.com/account

Bank Usage

Right click on an item's icon to view its context menu. If an item has an associated skin, the Wiki Search link will view the skin's wiki page. For banks of actual items (equipment, inventory, bank, and materials), the slots' coin price displayed is the sell price with tax (after 5% listing fee and 10% tax); for collections (skins, minis, dyes, catalog) it is the sell price without tax. Hover over a tradeable item's slot to view its current Buy Price (top) and Sell Price (bottom) without tax.

Search by Keyword

The case-insensitive search bar allows you to find items using space-separated keywords. It will look through every item's tooltip for any match of the words you type. For example, typing: "exo arm" will filter in any exotic armor pieces you have. If you want to look for an item that has toughness stats, then just type "toug" for partial matching of items with that keyword. You can search by item type by matching the words at the bottom of items' tooltips, such as "gizmo" (toys), "consumable" (foods), or "container" (bags).

Search by Price

Filter in items that are tradeable with a price by first typing either "<" or ">" then a period-separated coin string. For example, to search for items that can be sold for more than 1 gold on the Trading Post, type in the search ">1..". If you type ">1" then items valued above 1 silver will be shown. Here is the format for coin strings:

0.1 = 10
.01 = 1
1 = 1
1.55 = 1 55
2.4.7 = 2 4 7
2..6 = 2 0 6
230.6 = 2 30 60
920.. = 920

Unlockables Collection Usage

Unlockables are items that unlocks skins, minis, dyes, or other cosmetics to your account that you can reuse forever.

Click on an item icon to view the unlock's wiki page, or right click for more links; the Wiki Item link will view the best item to obtain that unlock. The coin price shown is the current Trading Post sell listing, which may be cheaper if you use buy orders instead.

Unlockable Acquisition

The slot icons show the type of payment required to acquire the item that has that unlockable:

  • Purchased from the Trading Post, or an NPC vendor. If priced 0 then it is acquired by random drop only.
  • Purchased from Renown Hearts or Cultural vendors.
  • Purchased from Laurel Merchants.
  • Purchased from the Gem Store.
  • Purchased from WvW masters.
  • Purchased from dungeon, map, or seasonal vendors.
  • Rewarded for fulfilling achievement requirements.
  • Rewarded for fulfilling GW1 achievements.
  • Created from crafting stations or Mystic Forge.
  • Rewarded for PvP tracks or achievements.
  • Endowed by character creation or development.

Wardrobe Usage

For fast page load, item icons and data are not downloaded and calculated until you have opened a tab to view that category of skins. Each icon shown is actually the item that unlocks that skin. Multiple items can have the same skin, so representative items were chosen based on these criteria: the item is most abundant and relatively cheap on the Trading Post, it should not be bindable so you can resell it after unlocking it, and for bound items it should be the most basic tier as to be cheaper from karma or cultural vendors.

Wardrobe Free-to-Apply Skins

If you have WvW Badges of Honor, spend them at the Weapon Master and Armor Master in your borderlands Citadel. The consumable skins offered can be applied without using a Transmutation Charge.

Dyes Palette Usage

Some dyes look better on different armor weights than others. Dyes are categorized into "Vibrant" (best for Light armor), "Leather" (Medium), and "Metallic" (Heavy). Use the search bar to filter in these dyes. You may also search by hue by typing a primary color: Gray, Brown, Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, or Purple.

Prices over the color squares (swatches) are sell listings, and are faded if you have unlocked that dye.

Catalog Usage

The Catalog is a virtual bank of the most commonly looted or used items in the game. The filled slots are items you have in your characters' inventory, bank, or materials storage; a slot's tooltip will show you where.

Custom Catalog

You may create, name, fill, and rearrange your own virtual bank tabs. Click the + icon to create a new tab, click the cog icon for the tab editor, and search an item's name and add it to the tab. Click on an item slot you added to move or delete it.

Cleanup Usage

This catalog shows salvageable items, unopened bags, stackable items, and likely "useless" items. If you are not sure if an item still has value, click its icon to read its wiki page before deciding to vendor/destroy it.

  • Salvage Items: use basic salvage kit or sell on TP
  • Ectoplasm Rares: use master or mystic salvage kit or sell on TP
  • Bags: open or sell on TP (if not bound)
  • Equipment Boxes: choose an item you are ready to use
  • Ascended Chests: choose an item you are ready to use
  • Food: distribute across your characters
  • Consumable: hover over the slots to see item stack count
  • Recipes: consume on your crafter character or sell on TP
  • Guild Decorations: trade at the Guild Hall Decorator NPC
  • Combine Items: combine, keep until enough, or destroy
  • Collection Items: keep until you have completed the collection, or vendor/destroy if you intend to never finish it
  • Junk: click the "Sell Junk" button at a vendor

Recipes Gallery Usage

The bank tabs contain craftable items for every discipline. A filled slot is the item associated with the recipe that you have learned on any character (although if you unlocked Light of Dwayna recipe on Tailor, it will show for Leatherworker and Armorsmith too because of the ArenaNet's API limitation). To find which of your characters know a recipe, use the bank search bar to search for the item produced from that recipe.
