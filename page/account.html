Privacy Statement

Last changed on: 2016-02-01

Knowing an API key allows anyone to view your account's ingame information, such as: characters and guilds, bank and inventory contents, gold and assets, achievements and unlocks, Trading Post and PvP match history, and playtime and activity. You may limit which of these type of information is exposed when creating an API key, and disallow further access to these information by deleting the API key at the ArenaNet website.

GW2Timer.com does not view, store, collect, monitor, or datamine API keys or your account information. When you enter the API key into the website, you will be downloading data from ArenaNet servers and performing calculations using this web application locally on your computer, and the keys are stored in your web browser, not a server.

GW2Timer.com uses Google Analytics and is able to view and store text that is shown on your address bar when you are on this website. If you do not wish to expose your API key, do not put your API key in the browser address bar. To share your key with other people, private message them the key and direct them to gw2timer.com/account

Account Audit

The audit feature will download, tally, and evaluate your possessions, unlocks, and trades data; estimate their bound value, calculate their Trading Post value, then present them in a table. This process will require almost all account permissions; your account information will be private between your computer and ArenaNet servers.

Account Summary

The giant coin numbers at the top of the audit report are your account's appraised and liquid value based on the "Sum & Conversion" table. Appraised is based on all items, currencies, and unlocks you have, using the Trading Post sell listing price without tax. Liquid is based on the items you can resell, plus the currencies in your wallet that can be converted to a tradeable item, with tax included.

Specifically, Appraised is the "Appraised Sell" value in the left "Account Sum" column added with all converted-to-coin values in the right "Account Sum" column, also it is added with the coin needed to buy the gems for the account upgrades (such as character slots). Liquid is the "Liquid Sell" value in the left "Account Sum" column added with some of the converted-to-coin values in the right "Wallet" column (liquidatable currencies like dungeon tokens to buy rares to salvage into tradeable Globs of Ectoplasm). Note that the "Account Sum" column is the sum of all the columns in the "Audit Categories" table.

Audit History

Every time you do an audit, your total appraised coin and gems, and the appraised sell value for each audit category will be compressed and saved in your browser. By default only the coin currency is recorded for each audit category, so for example the Outfits category (which consist of mostly gems) may appear as zero; turn on the convert currencies option and the history will consider all currencies, although the values will fluctuate more due to more variables such as gem price.

Older audit history will be overwritten if over the limit, which you can set in the Options page, Account section. You may also set the option to have the website automatically load an invisible iframe to this page at daily reset, so your daily audit history progresses.

Audit View

You can see every item that was audited by clicking the header cell on top of each audit category column. For example, click the "Bank" header cell in the "Audit Categories" table, which will create a virtual bank of the contents, showing the currency-converted appraised sell (untaxed) coin value of each item. If an item is not shown or does not have a coin value then it was not audited (its account bound payment was not pre-recorded or is unappraisable).

Auditing Details

  • Characters audit includes all items in equipment, slotted runes/sigils/infusions, inventory, and inventory bags (which themselves are items).
  • Slotted runes and sigils are appraised, while slotted tradeable infusions are considered liquid because they can be extracted without destroying the armor or weapon.
  • The Vault category includes guild banks from all guilds that the account owns.
  • Recipes audit is based on the associated recipe sheet's Trading Post price or non-coin currencies, if available.
  • Skins are not audited if the original item that unlocked the skin still exists. Skins that came from one item (for example Bunny Ears) but unlocks to the Light/Medium/Heavy collections will be audited once instead of three times. Precursor skins are not audited because their purpose is to create a legendary and not be an expensive skin.
  • Backpack-glider combos only have the backpack skin audited, and the "duplicate" glider will be ignored.
  • Boosters and gem store items rewarded from daily achievements are not audited.
  • Ascended gear's purpose is for their stats, so all of a type share the same appraisal. For example, ascended weapon and armor coin value is estimated by their T7 crafting materials along with Zojja inscription or insignia Trading Post price. Ascended backpacks all base their coin value on the stats-selectable forged backpack that cost 250 Vicious Claws.
  • Ascended rings dropped from fractals are not audited due to their abundance.
  • Legendary items use Trading Post price if available, otherwise they use the total price of their tradeable crafting ingredients. 77 Mystic Clovers assume it takes 250 Globs of Ectoplasm, Mystic Coins, and Obsidian Shards to forge.
  • Junk rarity are the only items to have vendor value considered.
  • For Trading Post transactions, Buying is the coin you'd receive back if you canceled all your orders; Selling is the coin values you'd receive if you canceled all your listings and relisted them at the current buy or sell prices.

Auditing Limitations

  • Only a few and most common untradeable items have a recorded currency, for example Infinite Mist Offensive Potion has Fractal Relics currency amount recorded (audited), but Gift of Ascension does not (not audited).
  • Untradeable items are appraised by one currency only, so if a raid vendor item cost 500 tokens and 20 gold, only the 500 tokens will be considered.
  • Account bound unopened bags and chest keys are not audited.

Prices and Values

Liquid value is coin price of items that can be sold on the Trading Post (no bound items), with 15% tax included. Appraised value is coin price of items that can be bought on the Trading Post (includes bound item), inherent vendor coin prices, or the sum prices of their tradeable crafting ingredients.

To have a fast audit, Trading Post prices of materials, skins, minis, dyes, and recipes are from a snapshot cache, while prices of bank, inventory, and equipment contents are fetched live from ArenaNet servers. Luxury items such as legendaries and permanent contracts always use the live Trading Post prices. If an item has no sell listing, it will use the highest buy order price instead.

Non-coin prices are from the representative lowest price of an item, such as karma needed to buy a low level Fine rarity armor that unlocks a skin, as opposed to a high level Masterwork rarity armor that unlocks the same skin but cost more karma.

Gem prices use the item's original price, not the sale discount price. If the gem item is available in a bulk discount, for example 5 items for 1000 gems, then the lowest price is used which is 1000/5 = 200 gems value for that item. If a gem skin is in a set of 6, then one piece is the set's price divided by 6.

Currency Conversions

Each type of currency can have its coin value approximated by taking a tradeable item it trades for and dividing its Trading Post price by that currency amount. For example, Pact Scout's Mapping Materials cost 5,250 karma and can be converted to a Vial of Powerful Blood (let it be 50 silver); so 5000 copper / 5250 karma ≈ 0.952 copper per 1 karma. Note that the Trading Post price is the sell price, without tax.

Dungeon tokens' coin value is determined by the price of Glob of Ectoplasm, which for the sake of simplicity assumes 100% droprate from the 30 tokens needed to buy a Rare dungeon armor piece to salvage. The barter-conversion rates are stored in this file, Ctrl+F the word "conversion".

The coin value next to the gem currency is the amount needed to convert to those gems. Money value (USD/Euro) is the amount needed to buy gems to convert to those coins.

Account Achievements

The total account Achievement Points must be "manually" calculated from each participated achievements, added with the repeatable and capped achievements such as Daily and Salvage Master. Some achievements may be missing from ArenaNet's API, so the total AP displayed may be lower than your actual.

Use the search bar to preview achievements, to view the category an achievement is in, or to filter the currently visible achievement "bank slots".

The achievements that cycle daily, such as fractal tiers, Lake Doric events, or Halloween Candy Craver are not tracked by ArenaNet so will appear as unfinished.

Bank Usage

Right click on an item's icon to view its context menu. For banks of actual items (equipment, inventory, bank, and materials), the slots' coin price displayed is the sell price with tax (after 5% listing fee and 10% tax); for collections (skins, minis, dyes, catalog) it is the sell price without tax. Hover over a tradeable item's slot or use the tradeable filter to view the current Buy Price (top) and Sell Price (bottom) without tax.

Search by Keyword

The case-insensitive search bar allows you to find items using space-separated keywords. It will look through every item's tooltip for any match of the words you type. For example, typing: "exo arm" will filter in any exotic armor pieces you have. If you want to look for an item that has toughness stats, then just type "toug" for partial matching of items with that keyword. You can search by item type by matching the words at the bottom of items' tooltips, such as "gizmo" (toys), "consumable" (foods), or "container" (bags). For catalogs, you can search using your characters' names or places where the items are found.

Search by Price

Starting your search with one equality sign "<" or ">" will search by Buy Price (buy orders); two equality signs "<<" or ">>" will search by Sell Price (sell listings). Type a period-separated coin string after the equality sign(s) to do the search.

For example, to search for items that can be instantly sold for more than 1 gold on the Trading Post, type in the search ">1..", or for one silver type ">1". Alternately, type ">>5.70." to search for items that can be listed for 5 gold and 70 silver or higher. Here is the format for coin strings:

0.1 = 10
.01 = 1
1 = 1
1.55 = 1 55
2.4.7 = 2 4 7
2..6 = 2 0 6
230.6 = 2 30 60
920.. = 920

For searching by gem prices, just enter the whole number without any period: "<500" search for items less than 500 gems.

Guild Bank Usage

Vaults are divided into subvaults (max 3 subvaults/tabs per guild), and the vaults with the most unique items are ordered at the top.

Unlockables Collection Usage

Unlockables are items that unlocks skins, minis, dyes, or other cosmetics to your account that you can reuse forever.

Click on an item icon to view the unlock's wiki page, or right click for more links; the Wiki Item link will view the best item to obtain that unlock. The coin price shown is the current Trading Post sell listing, which may be cheaper if you use buy orders instead.

Unlockable Acquisition

  • Purchased from Renown Hearts or Cultural vendors.
  • Purchased from Laurel Merchants.
  • Purchased from the Gem Store.
  • Purchased from WvW masters or rewarded from WvW tracks.
  • Purchased from dungeon, map, or seasonal vendors.
  • Rewarded from achievement requirements.
  • Rewarded from GW1 achievements.
  • Created from crafting stations or Mystic Forge.
  • Rewarded from PvP tracks or achievements.
  • Purchased from PvP vendors.
  • Endowed by character creation or development.

Wardrobe Usage

For fast page load, item icons and data are not downloaded and calculated until you have opened a tab to view that category of skins. Each icon shown is actually the item that unlocks that skin. Multiple items can have the same skin, so representative items were chosen based on these criteria: the item is most abundant and relatively cheap on the Trading Post, it should not be bindable so you can resell it after unlocking it, and for bound items it should be the most basic tier as to be cheaper from karma or cultural vendors.

Wardrobe Free-to-Apply Skins

If you have WvW Badges of Honor, spend them at the Weapon Master and Armor Master in your borderlands Citadel. The consumable skins offered can be applied without using a Transmutation Charge.

Dyes Palette Usage

Some dyes look better on different armor weights than others. Dyes are categorized into "Vibrant" (best for Light armor), "Leather" (Medium), and "Metallic" (Heavy). Use the search bar to filter in these dyes. You may also search by hue by typing a primary color: Gray, Brown, Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, or Purple.

Prices over the color squares (swatches) are sell listings, and are faded if you have unlocked that dye.

Home Instance Cats

Each unlockable cat is represented in the "bank slot" as the food you feed it. The cat # is its ID in the ArenaNet API. Use the collectible map or visit the wiki page for acquisition of cats.

Possessions Usage

All items you have in your bank, inventories, equipment, and material storage locations are displayed here. You may search for an item by typing its item name, rarity, type, tooltip keywords, character name, or location.

Catalog Usage

The Catalog is a virtual bank of the most commonly looted or used items in the game. The filled slots are items you have in your characters' inventory, bank, or materials storage; a slot's tooltip will show you where.

Custom Catalog

You may create, name, fill, and rearrange your own virtual bank tabs. Click the + icon to create a new tab, click the cog icon for the tab editor, and search an item's name and add it to the tab. Click on an item slot you added to move or delete it.

Cleanup Usage

This catalog shows salvageable items, unopened bags, unused consumables, and likely "useless" items. If you are not sure if an item is worth keeping, click its icon to read its wiki page before deciding to vendor/destroy it. Sell prices shown includes tax.

  • Salvage Items: use basic salvage kit or sell on TP
  • Ectoplasm Rares: use master or mystic salvage kit or sell on TP
  • Unopened Bags: open or sell on TP (if not bound)
  • Equipment Boxes: choose an item you are ready to use
  • Ascended Chests: choose an item you are ready to use
  • Food: distribute across your characters
  • Consumable: hover over slots to see character stack count
  • Skin: apply when you are ready to use
  • Recipes: consume on your crafter character or sell on TP
  • Guild Decorations: trade at the Guild Hall Decorator NPC
  • Combine Items: combine, keep until enough, or destroy
  • Collection Items: keep until you have completed the collection, or vendor/destroy if you intend to never finish it
  • Junk: click the "Sell Junk" button at a vendor

Recipes Gallery Usage

The bank tabs contain craftable items for every discipline. A filled slot is the item crafted by the recipe that you have learned on any character. To find which of your characters know a recipe, use the bank search bar to search and click on an item crafted by that recipe.

Gem Store Wishlist

Hover over an item and click on to subscribe/unsubscribe to it. Faded slots are items not yet available on the gem store, and subscribing will alert when they return. Bright slots are available items, so alerts will be when they are discounted in price. Slot highlight colors: Blue—promoted items (or recently added), Gold—discounted items.

By the top search bar is the button to filter what items are: available, subscribed, and unavailable (1st, 2nd, 3rd click).

The coin value over the gem value is how much gold is needed to exchange into that item's gems.

Gem Store Alerts

The alert is a spoken voice; you may enable/disable and set the volume on the map pane under the icon. The checks for available or discounted items happen when you first load this website, and every quarter hour as long as you have the website in a browser tab.

Item Museum

A chronological bank of items released in ArenaNet's API. This allows you to see most new items from the game's patches and releases. History from 2013-2016 are based on GW2.FR's database.

Pact Supply

Gallery of the recipe sheets offered by Pact Supply Network Agents throughout one year (366 days, starting from December 2015 to December 2016). The stack count is the number of times the recipe was offered. The offers are random, and they do not necessarily repeat on the same day of the year. View the history in raw format. Use the dashboard on the main page labeled "Pact Supply" to view the current NPC vendor map locations to purchase these items.

Trading Post Transactions Usage

Transactions are items you are buying, selling, have bought, or have sold on the Trading Post. The info button next to each tab header will print out a chronological list of items transacted for that month.

Transactions Timestamps

For "Buying" and "Selling" transactions, the timestamp is when you created that transaction, and the countdown in parentheses is how long since then till now. For "Bought" and "Sold" transactions, the timestamp is when the item was successfully bought or sold (not when ordered or listed), and the countdown is the time it took for the item to be bought or sold.

Transactions Prices

For "Buying" and "Bought" transactions, prices on the slots are the sum of all transactions for that item for that month, without tax. For "Selling" and "Sold" transactions, it is the same but with tax included, and the faded prices over tabs are the pre-taxed value. You may hover over the slots or use the tradeable filter to see the item's current Buy (top) and Sell (bottom) prices.

Transactions Outbid/Outprice

For the "Buying" section, if no one has placed a higher bid on an item than you (not outbidden), then that item will be highlighted green. For the "Selling" section, if no one is selling an item lower than you (not outpriced), then that item will be highlighted green.
